Bearable Savings

Bearable Savings is a mobile app that helps you meet saving goals and hooks you up with online microgigs for extra paw-ket change. Even though this app is for anyone who wants to save money, use has been targeted to teenagers and college kids that need help saving for school needs or the next fun adventure in life.

Onboarding

These initial screens seek to create a smooth and effective onboarding experience while quickly communicating important features of the app with some friendly and cute illustrations.

Research & Inspiration

Studying design principals, flow patterns and heuristics of other banking and saving money apps helped provide a basis with which to move forward

User Flows & Wireframing

This phase was essential in starting conversations with potential users to get their invaluable insight and brainstorm ideas and improvements for optimal usability

User Testing & Feedback

User testing and feedback helped inform the app and allowed for meaningful modifications making the product delightful to use

Visual Problem Solving

Color, typography and visual hierarchy were added and reiterated several times to find the best possible visual solution for smooth navigation

Wireframing

Throughout my coursework with CareerFoundry I’ve learned the importance of this initial, raw presentation of ideas and how its purpose is not to impress users but to have them impress us with their insight.

This phase opens up conversations about depicting concepts, designs, and layouts among many other important topics.

Home Screens

Home is a place where data on the user’s finances (how much money they spend and on what) is displayed as well as what their goals are and how much time they have to meet them.

Savings Screens

Saving money can be tough, especially when you don’t have a lot of time to do so. By providing personalized information on how a user can save based on their actual finances, the tool itself can provide financial advice during these finite periods of saving.

Find a Microgig

There are lots of microgig possibilities online and putting them together in one place can help users keep track of what kinds of opportunities they can take to get a little paw-ket change.
