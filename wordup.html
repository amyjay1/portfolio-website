A Language App Case Study

Word up focuses on a handful of practical features including a dictionary, worldwide pronunciation variations, collocations, and the ability to make flashcards. This app isn’t about bells and whistles: it’s about simplicity, usability and functionalism.

Process

Challenges

This app seeks to keep information organized while keeping things simple. It attempts to present the vast amount of interesting details and facts about words, yet also providing the simplest flashcard making process possible.

Wireframing

Several iterations of wireframing and prototyping were carried out to reach the best visual and user friendly solution possible.

User testing and Prototyping

Receiving constructive feedback from users during this phase provided invaluable suggestions, which helped to create effectively aesthetic and smooth design solutions.

Some users were confused by the first screen design, which attempted to evoke the feeling of a traditional typewriter, but led them to expect they could interact with it. This feedback inspired idea of “painting” with letters from different languages, which users preferred in A/B testing.

Initially some users reported feeling frustrated by the use of abstract letters as icons, which could impair smooth navigation. Further testing led to the creation of an alternate screen. Subsequent A/B testing provided no clear preference and ultimately the original screen was chosen as it felt more on brand with the app.
